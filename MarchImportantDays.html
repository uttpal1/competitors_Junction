Important Days of March

Here we are going to learn about important days (National and International) of March that will be useful for the student preparing for the government and other competitive exam like UPSC SSC STATE PCS Railway Bank.Every year 2-3 questions are asked from this topic in every government exam.We have briefly discuss the reason behind celebration.Before appearing for any government exam make sure to have a look at list of important date and days.

1st March: Zero Discrimination Day

Zero Discrimination Day, is an annual day celebrated by the UN and other international organisations. The day aims to promote equality before the law and in practice throughout all of the member countries of the UN.

3rd March: World Wildlife Day

The United Nations General Assembly (UNGA) on December 20, 2013, at its 68th session, decided to proclaim March 3, the day of the adoption of the Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species of Wild Fauna and Flora (CITES), asWorld Wildlife Day

4th March: National Security Day

March 4 is observed as National Security day to praise and respect the work of our security forces. The day is also known as Rashtriya Suraksha Diwas. This day is dedicated to all the security forces such as policemen, commandos, guards, army officers, para-military forces and other persons.

8th March: International Women's Day

International Women's Day is celebrated on 8th March every year. The day celebrates womanhood all across the globe. Women's Day celebrates the achievements of women in different fields. The day also focuses on gender equality and women's rights.

11th March: World Kidney Day

World Kidney Day, s a global health awareness campaign focusing on the importance of the kidneys and reducing the frequency and impact of kidney disease and its associated health problems worldwide. World Kidney Day is observed annually on the 2nd Thursday in March.

14th March: International Day of Action for Rivers

International Day of Action for Rivers , is celebrated on 14 March to raise awareness about the importance of rivers and climate change.It is a day when diverse communities and individuals around the globe come together to raise voice against the destructive water development projects, health and sustainable management of the watersheds. The day was earlier known as International Day against Dams, for Rivers, Water, and Life. In India also several projects are started for the conservation of rivers or against dam construction. The Narmada Bachao Andolan was one of them.

15th March: World Consumer Rights Day

World Consumer Rights Day, is a means of raising global awareness about consumer rights and needs. Celebrating the day is a chance to demand that the rights of all consumers are respected and protected, and to protest against market abuses and social injustices which undermine those rights.

20th March: International Day of Happiness

International Day of Happiness, is celebrated throughout the world on the 20th of March. It was established by the United Nations General Assembly on 28 June 2012. International Day of Happiness, recognizing the relevance of happiness and well-being as universal goals and aspirations in the lives of human beings around the world and the importance of their recognition in public policy objectives.

20th March: World Sparrow Day

World Sparrow Day, is a day designated to raise awareness of the house sparrow and then other common birds to urban environments, and of threats to their populations,.

22nd March: World Water Day

World Water Day, is an annual UN observance day that highlights the importance of freshwater. The day is used to advocate for the sustainable management of freshwater resources.

23rd March: World Meteorological Day

World Meteorological Day, is observed on March 23 every year; the day was established at the first International Meteorological Congress in Vienna, Austria, in 1873 and on March 23, 1950, International Meteorological Organization became the World Meteorological Organization.

24th March:World TB Day

World TB Day observed on 24 March each year, is designed to build public awareness about the global epidemic of tuberculosis and efforts to eliminate the disease.

28th March:World Theatre Day

World Theatre Day is an international observance celebrated on 27 March. It was initiated in 1961 by the International Theatre Institute.World Theatre Day raises the importance of theatre arts, how they played an important role in the field of entertainment and the changes that theatre brings in life.
